Burns & Scalds

Quick steps to treat burns before medical help arrives.

Types of Burns

First-Degree Burns
Red, minor swelling, and pain affecting only the top layer of skin.
Second-Degree Burns
Blisters, deeper skin damage, severe pain, and swelling.
Third-Degree Burns
White, charred skin; damage goes through all skin layers.

First Aid Steps

  1. Cool the burn under running water for 10–20 minutes.
  2. Remove any tight clothing or jewelry near the burn.
  3. Cover with a sterile, non-fluffy dressing.
  4. Seek medical help for severe burns.

What NOT to Do

  • ❌ Do not use ice directly.
  • ❌ Do not pop blisters.
  • ❌ Do not apply oils, butter, or creams.
