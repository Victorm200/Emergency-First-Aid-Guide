Why First Aid Matters

Knowing basic first aid can save lives before professional help arrives. Learn the steps to handle emergencies with confidence.

❤️ CPR
  • Check for responsiveness by tapping and shouting.
  • If unresponsive and not breathing, call emergency services immediately.
  • Begin chest compressions: push hard and fast (100–120 per minute).
  • After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if trained.
  • Continue until help arrives or the person starts breathing.
🔥 Burns
  • Cool under running water for 10–20 minutes.
  • Remove jewelry or clothing near the burn (unless stuck).
  • Cover with a clean, non-stick dressing.
  • Do NOT apply ice, butter, oil, or ointments.
  • Seek medical help if severe or on face/hands/genitals.
🩸 Bleeding
  • Wear gloves if available.
  • Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • If blood soaks through, add more layers — don’t remove the first.
  • Keep injured part raised above the heart.
  • Call emergency services if bleeding is severe or unstoppable.
🦴 Fractures
  • Do not move the injured area unnecessarily.
  • Stabilize using a splint or padding if trained.
  • Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling.
  • Do not try to realign the bone.
  • Call emergency help immediately.
😮‍💨 Choking
  • If the person can’t speak, cough, or breathe — act quickly.
  • Give 5 back blows between shoulder blades.
  • If not effective, perform 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich).
  • Repeat until the object is expelled or they become unresponsive.
  • If unresponsive, start CPR and call for help.
🐍 Snake Bite
  • Keep the person calm and still.
  • Keep the bitten limb below heart level.
  • Do not suck the venom or cut the wound.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
🌊 Drowning
  • Remove the person from the water.
  • Check breathing and pulse.
  • If not breathing, begin CPR immediately.
  • Call for emergency help right away.
