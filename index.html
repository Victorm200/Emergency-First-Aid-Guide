Why First Aid Matters

Knowing basic first aid can save lives. Learn the simple steps you can take before professional help arrives.

❤️ CPR
  • ❤️ CPR
    • Check for responsiveness by tapping and shouting.
    • If unresponsive and not breathing, call emergency services immediately.
    • Begin chest compressions: place hands in the center of the chest and push hard and fast (about 100–120 compressions per minute).
    • After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if trained. Tilt the head back, pinch the nose, and blow into the mouth.
    • Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives or the person starts breathing.
  • 🔥 Burns
    • Cool the burn under running water for at least 10–20 minutes immediately.
    • Remove any clothing or jewelry near the burned area, unless stuck to the skin.
    • Cover the burn with a clean, non-stick dressing or cloth.
    • Do NOT apply ice, butter, oil, or ointments to the burn.
    • Seek medical help if the burn is deep, covers a large area, or is on the face, hands, or genitals.
    🩸 Bleeding
  • 🩸 Bleeding & Wound Care
    • Put on gloves if available to protect yourself.
    • Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or bandage.
    • If blood soaks through, do not remove the cloth. Add more layers and keep pressing.
    • Keep the injured part raised above the heart if possible.
    • Call emergency services if the bleeding is severe, does not stop, or if there's an embedded object.
    🦴 Fractures
  • 🦴 Fractures & Broken Bones
    • Do not move the affected area unnecessarily to prevent more damage.
    • Stabilize the injured part using a splint or padding if trained.
    • Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling and pain.
    • Do not attempt to realign the bone or push it back in place.
    • Call for emergency help and keep the person calm and still.
    😮‍💨 Choking
  • 🚨 Choking
    • Ask the person if they’re choking. If they can’t speak, cough, or breathe, act quickly.
    • Stand behind them and give 5 back blows between their shoulder blades using the heel of your hand.
    • If this doesn’t work, perform 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver): wrap arms around the waist and press into the upper stomach with a quick inward thrust.
    • Repeat the cycle of 5 back blows and 5 thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unresponsive.
    • If they become unresponsive, begin CPR and call for emergency help immediately.
    🔥 Burns
    • Cool the burn under running water for at least 10 minutes.
    • Cover with a clean, non-stick bandage or cloth.
    • Do NOT apply ice, oils, or creams.
    • Call emergency services if burn is severe or on the face, hands, or genitals.
    🐍 Snake Bite
    • Keep the person still and calm.
    • Keep the bitten area below heart level.
    • Do not suck the venom or cut the wound.
    • Call emergency services immediately.
    🌊 Drowning
    • Remove the person from water.
    • Check for breathing and pulse.
    • If no breathing, begin CPR immediately.
    • Call for help right away.
